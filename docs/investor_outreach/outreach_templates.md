# Cold Outreach Templates — ChairMatch + Alltagsengel Pre-Seed

**Voice rules:**
- German-direct, American-pitchy. Confident, not arrogant.
- Never use: *disrupt, synergy, revolutionize, AI-powered* (as a standalone phrase), *we're the X of Y*, *cutting-edge*.
- Lead with the most specific, hardest-to-fake fact in sentence one. Save the ask for the last sentence.
- Numbers > adjectives. Specifics > thesis-speak.
- 2026 cold outreach — assume the investor reads on a phone, between two other emails.

---

## Template A — LinkedIn DM (3 sentences)

> Hi [First name] — Yusuf, Frankfurt. I'm building two pre-seed marketplaces on one Next.js+Supabase stack — ChairMatch (salon chair rentals, B2B) and Alltagsengel (§45b-reimbursable home-care, live in App Store + Play Store with first paying user). Saw your [recent investment in X / Tweet on Y / [Firm] thesis on Z] — would value 15 minutes if you're taking pre-seed cold reads. Deck ready if useful.

**Variants:**
- For pure-marketplace investors → swap second clause: *"two-platform marketplace bet built on one stack — chair rentals + reimbursable home-care, both live in DACH."*
- For care/aging investors → swap second clause: *"a German home-care marketplace that bills directly to Pflegekasse under §45b — live in App Store, first paying booking through clean. Built alongside a sister B2B marketplace on the same stack."*
- For accelerators → close with: *"Considering [YC W26 / Antler Berlin / Speedrun]. Worth a conversation?"*

**Character budget:** ~ 480 chars. Fits LinkedIn DM and X DM cleanly.

## Template B — Cold email (8 sentences)

**Subject line options (rotate, A/B test):**
- `Frankfurt pre-seed — two marketplaces, one stack, first booking live`
- `§45b SGB XI + chair rentals — cold note from a solo founder in Frankfurt`
- `[FirstName] — quick note on [Portfolio Co X] adjacency from Frankfurt`
- `Pre-seed, DACH, regulated service marketplaces — one founder`

**Body:**

> Hi [First name],

**Notes:**
- Keep the body under 220 words. The version above is ~ 215 words.
- Replace `[month]` with the actual month of first booking before sending.
- The `[1–2 personalization sentences]` is the make-or-break — see Template C.

## Template C — Personalization anchors (how to spend 90 seconds before each send)

For each investor on the longlist, find ONE of the following and write a 1–2 sentence reference. Spend more than 5 minutes on this and you're wasting time; spend less than 60 seconds and the email reads generic.

**Anchor priority (use the first one you find):**

1. **Recent portfolio company adjacency** *(strongest)*
   - "I noticed [Firm] led [Portfolio Co X]'s pre-seed in [month/year] — they're solving [adjacent problem] in [adjacent market]. The reimbursement rail in Alltagsengel sits in the same regulatory neighborhood, and ChairMatch matches their [supply-aggregation / two-sided trust / vertical SaaS] motion."
   - Example for **Primetime Partners** (backed Duos): *"I saw Primetime backed Duos around their pivot to AI-personal-assistants-for-elders — Alltagsengel sits one step earlier in the flow: we book the physical care helper, route the invoice to Pflegekasse, and become the assistant's transaction layer."*
   - Example for **FJ Labs** (Fabrice Grinda): *"Two-sided service marketplaces in regulated DACH categories isn't well-represented in your 1000+ marketplace map yet — happy to run the unit econ on a 15-min call."*

2. **Published thesis / podcast / blog** *(very strong)*
   - "Your [thesis post / podcast with X / Twitter thread on Y] on [specific point] is exactly the framing I'm building toward."
   - Example for **Speedinvest Marketplaces (Mathias Ockenfels)**: *"Your Marketplace Scorecard maps onto ChairMatch almost line-by-line — happy to walk through the score honestly, including where I'm still weak."*
   - Example for **Cyan Banister / Long Journey**: *"You've made the 'unsexy job-creation' bet your whole career — Alltagsengel creates formal, insurance-billable income for care helpers who currently work cash-in-hand. Different decade, same insight."*

3. **Funding/launch news from last 90 days** *(good)*
   - "Congrats on [Fund X close / new partner announcement / portfolio exit] — saw it on [source]. Sending while it's top of mind."

4. **Conference / talk reference** *(good)*
   - "Caught your [Slush / SaaStr / TechCrunch Disrupt / DLD / OMR] panel on [topic] — your point about [specific point] is why I'm writing."

5. **Shared geography / background hook** *(weakest, only if above unavailable)*
   - "[Frankfurt-based ops background / DACH thesis / German healthtech pattern]." Use sparingly.

**Hard rules:**
- One anchor per email. Never two.
- Reference a specific portfolio company, post date, or quote. *"I love your investments"* is dead on arrival.
- If you can't find one of the five anchors above in 90 seconds, **move that investor to a lower priority tranche.** Cold-outreach math punishes generic emails harder than missed sends.

## Cadence playbook

- **Tranche 1 — top 10** (see report): send within first 5 business days of campaign. Hand-written personalization, no template shortcuts.
- **Tranche 2 — next 30**: send within 2 weeks. Template B with 90-sec personalization.
- **Tranche 3 — remainder**: rolling, batch-send 5/day. Template A on LinkedIn first; if no response in 4 days, Template B by email.
- **Follow-up rule:** *exactly* one follow-up, 5–7 business days after first send, max 3 sentences. No third touch unless they engaged in some way. Pre-seed angels remember spammers.

**Follow-up template (the only one you ever send):**

> Hi [First name] — circling back on my note about ChairMatch + Alltagsengel. Quick update for context: [one specific number that changed since first send — e.g., "+3 Engels onboarded this week" or "ChairMatch private beta now live in Rhein-Main"]. Still happy to send the deck if useful.

## Subject-line testing matrix (track open rates)

Set up a 4×2 A/B early. Two batches of 20 cold emails, four subject-line variants each:

| Variant | Hypothesis | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| `Frankfurt pre-seed — two marketplaces, one stack, first booking live` | Specifics + geography sell | Default |
| `§45b SGB XI + chair rentals — cold note from a solo founder in Frankfurt` | Curiosity gap — they'll Google §45b | For care-tech investors |
| `[FirstName] — quick note on [Portfolio Co X] adjacency from Frankfurt` | Personalization in subject | When anchor is portfolio-co |
| `Pre-seed, DACH, regulated service marketplaces — one founder` | Thesis-anchored | For marketplace generalists |

Track in a simple spreadsheet: investor name, channel (LI/email), subject variant, sent date, opened, replied, outcome. After 40 sends you'll have a signal. Pre-seed open rates: a 25%+ reply rate on personalized first sends is a strong campaign; <10% means the anchor work is too thin.

## Don'ts (real ones)

- **Don't open with "I hope this finds you well."** — instant delete.
- **Don't attach the deck on first send.** Link it. Track opens. Attachments trigger spam filters and look amateur.
- **Don't apologize for being solo, Schufa-flagged, or pre-revenue.** State it as a fact in the right slide. Apology smells like fear.
- **Don't mass-mention every portfolio company they have.** Pick one. Be specific.
- **Don't use bullet lists in cold emails.** Prose only — bullets read as templated.
- **Don't send Sunday or Friday afternoon.** Tue/Wed/Thu 8–11am investor local time → highest open rate.
